Basic Probability Beginner

üí° Understanding Probability

Probability is always between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%):

Rolling a standard die: - Probability of rolling a 3: 1/6 (one favorable outcome out of 6) - Probability of rolling even: 3/6 = 1/2 (three favorable: 2,4,6) - Probability of rolling less than 7: 6/6 = 1 (certain) - Probability of rolling 7: 0/6 = 0 (impossible) Key: P(event) = (ways it can happen) / (total ways)

üí° The Key Difference

Understanding when order matters is crucial:

üí° Why Factorials Count Arrangements

Factorials count the number of ways to arrange n distinct objects:

Why 0! = 1? There's exactly one way to arrange zero objects: do nothing!

üí° The Casework Method

When direct counting is hard, break into cases:

Complementary Counting Advanced

Complementary Counting

üí° When to Use Complements

Some problems are easier to count what DOESN'T happen:

Rolling two dice, probability of getting at least one 6? HARD WAY: Count all ways to get at least one 6 - First die is 6: (6,1), (6,2), ..., (6,6) = 6 ways - Second die is 6: (1,6), (2,6), ..., (5,6) = 5 ways - Careful not to double-count (6,6)! EASY WAY: Use complement - Total outcomes: 6 √ó 6 = 36 - No 6's: 5 √ó 5 = 25 - At least one 6: 36 - 25 = 11 Probability: 11/36

Look for "at least," "at most," or "not all" as clues to use complements!
